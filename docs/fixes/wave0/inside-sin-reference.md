## Bosses

### Seymour Omnis (p.74)
- **HP**: 80,000 (Overkill: 15,000)
- **AP**: 24,000 (36,000 Overkill)
- **Weakness**: Varies by elemental disc position
- **Steal**: Shining Gem, Supreme Gem
- **Mechanics**:
  - Four spinning discs behind him show the four elements; the circle closest to him sets his elemental type
  - If all four are red, he absorbs Fire and casts four Firaga spells each turn — making him weak to Ice
  - Targeting the discs with attacks or spells forces them to rotate; mixing types weakens his spells but removes his clear weakness
  - Ideal play: Auron uses Mental Break + Armor Break; Lulu blasts with his weak element; hasted characters swap at least two discs to mix his types
  - Alternatively: summon an aeon matching his current element — Seymour's spells heal it
  - Kimahri's Mighty Guard adds Shell + elemental Nul shields; Lulu's Phantom Bangle (absorbs three elements) is excellent here
  - ~Halfway through: Seymour casts Dispel on the party; his next action will be the non-elemental **Ultima** spell
  - To survive Ultima: swap in characters with 5,000+ HP, or summon an expendable aeon to absorb it

### Braska's Final Aeon — Jecht (p.75)
- **HP**: 60,000 (First Form) / 120,000 (Second Form)
- **AP**: 0
- **Weakness**: None
- **Steal**: Turbo Ether
- **Mechanics**:
  - Yu Pagodas try to heal Jecht each turn — do NOT waste time disabling them
  - Apply Zombie Attack on Jecht with Auron so the Pagodas damage him with each healing attempt; reapply regularly as the effect wears off
  - Trigger Command: "Talk to Jecht" during Tidus's turns to drain his Overdrive gauge significantly; works only a couple of times — save it until the gauge is nearly full
  - Let Jecht use his Overdrive at least once before talking him out of subsequent ones (guide recommends experiencing it)
  - If Overdrive cannot be prevented, summon an aeon to absorb it
  - Second Form activates after 60,000 HP lost: use Mighty Guard, Rikku's Mighty G mix, or Auto-Life on key characters
  - Finish strong: unload Overdrives, Mega-Potions on the zombified boss, Spare Change (100,000 gil = 9,999 damage), aeon Overdrives
  - Further battles follow but "it's impossible to lose them" — carry no items into new game, so use everything

## Walkthrough Prose

### Sea of Sorrow (p.74)
After defeating Sin on the airship exterior, the ship enters Sin's shell. You can still leave at any time to pursue side quests. The Sea of Sorrow map only shows the immediate area around you — use guide maps to navigate. Treasure chests sit at the tops of waterfalls; you can ride waterfalls down but not climb them, requiring circuitous routes to reach the chests.

Enemies here are extremely powerful and yield considerable AP. The most dangerous is the **Behemoth King**, which casts Meteor on the entire party when it dies (~6,000 damage to all). Options to handle Meteor:
- Cast Protect (not Shell — Protect specifically halves physical/magical splash) before the killing blow
- Let an aeon take the final hit
- Deliver the killing blow with a Counterattack weapon or a "Catch" weapon to prevent the Meteor
- Simply flee this encounter

### City of Dying Dreams (p.75)
Past the Save Sphere, search the right-hand wall for a glyph. Kill 10 fiends in random encounters to open the first door; a second door requires more kills; a third demands 15 kills. Total: 35 random encounter kills. Reward: 1 Lv. 4 Key Sphere.

When fighting **Demonolith**: equip Stoneproof armor on a front-line character, or fight exclusively with aeons — Demonolith's Breath attack can petrify the entire party for an instant game over.

The final Save Sphere in the game is near the top of the ramp leading out of the City. **This is the last chance to return to the airship and pursue side quests before the finale.**

### Tower of Death (p.75)
The Tower of Death crashes to the ground as you proceed north from the final Save Sphere. Enter it. Inside, icicles shoot upward from the ground — a battle triggers if one hits you. Navigation tip: stand still until the camera rotates to show the area better; move away if a white glow appears under Tidus's feet.

Small colored crystals appear and fade — run to touch them before they vanish to collect the items inside (mostly weapons and rare spheres). Collect 10 items to trigger the final showdown.

## Tips

- **Protect vs Shell for Behemoth King Meteor**: Use Protect, not Shell. Shell reduces magical damage; Behemoth King's Meteor is treated as a special/splash attack that Protect halves.
- **Aeon tanking**: Summoning an aeon to absorb Behemoth King's death Meteor, Seymour's Ultima, or Jecht's Overdrive is the safest approach when you can afford it.
- **Zombie trick on Jecht**: The Zombie Attack + Pagoda synergy is the most efficient damage strategy for Braska's Final Aeon. Prioritize Auron's turn early in the fight.
- **Spare Change**: With 100,000 gil, Spare Change deals 9,999 damage. If you have excess money, use it here — you can't carry items or money to a new game.
- **Laevatein exit**: After sliding into the pit containing Laevatein, move along the wall next to the chest until the prompt appears to climb out.

## Missable Warnings

- **City of Dying Dreams glyph doors**: You must kill fiends in the immediate area to open them. If you run past, the reward (Lv. 4 Key Sphere) is not available later.
- **Final Save Sphere**: The guide explicitly calls this "your last chance to return to the airship and pursue side quests before the finale." Everything after this is a point of no return for side content.
- **Tower of Death crystals**: The colored crystal items fade quickly — if you miss them during navigation there is no second pass. The guide does note they are "of little use at this late point."
